Career spans mine rehabilitation monitoring at METServe, aquatic habitat mapping and AUSRIVAS bioassessment at Hydrobiology, multispectral remote sensing and EA approval mapping at Geoconsult, and native title, vegetation, and place names spatial work at Queensland Government. Each role involved producing outputs that fed directly into regulatory decisions — court submissions, EIA documentation, legislative compliance.

Academic path ran from natural sciences at Bologna across biological, earth, and environmental disciplines, to marine science and environmental management at UQ. Research covered Diamantina Basin carbon sequestration using HYDRUS-2D and Great Barrier Reef carbonate chemistry under ANZG ecotoxicological frameworks.

Multilingual across Chinese, English, Italian, German, and Spanish. Volunteered since 2015 translating scientific documentary content into Chinese. Reef Census surveys across Moreton Bay and the Whitsundays. COP15 CBD reporting for Chinese and African civil society organisations in Kunming.

Approach

Every role has involved being dropped into a system that was already complicated — a mine site, a river catchment, a government database, a court boundary dispute — and finding the question that actually mattered inside it. The domain keeps changing. The pattern does not.

Most GIS people have never logged a core or identified a macroinvertebrate. Most field ecologists cannot build an FME workflow. The gap between those two worlds is where the interesting work happens. Spatial tools are the medium. The point is what the data is arguing.
